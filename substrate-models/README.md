# The Substrate Models

Weighted node graph models of the human cognitive substrate — built in Observable HQ.

These are not early sketches. They are the technically serious objects in this body of work.

## What They Are

A weighted graph model of the 28+ human cognitive capacities that AI systems depend on:

- **Nodes** — individual substrate capacities (Meaning, Persuasion, Social, Culture, Emotion, Judgment, Cognition, Risk & Reality Negotiation, Coordination & Social Physics, Meaning Over Time, Signal vs Noise Intelligence, Creation Under Constraint)
- **Edges** — relationships between capacities
- **Weights** — extraction rates and dependency strength
- **Roles** — the human roles that maintain each capacity (journalists, teachers, editors, moderators, parents, artists, nurses, farmers, coders)

## The Many-to-Many Relationship

Individual roles maintain multiple capacities.  
Individual capacities are maintained by multiple roles.

This many-to-many structure makes the dependency concrete and undeniable. Remove the role, degrade the capacity. Degrade the capacity, degrade the model.

## As Simulation Instruments

These models can function as:

- **Training models** for AI alignment research — run scenarios, observe substrate degradation
- **Game mechanics** for policy simulation — what happens if extraction continues at current rates?
- **Demonstration tools** for foundation and think tank pitches
- **Research instruments** for complexity science applications
